WHAT IS HORIZONS

JUNE 12th, 2024

Lets get into it,

NFTs took the world by storm back in 2020 and had a major shockwave in not only the tech industry, but the art industry as a whole. People were both fascinated and apalled with this new phenomenon and it was very difficult to go through the internet without seeing a hearing someone mention NFTs. However since then, NFTs have taken a backseat and are currently waiting for their next resurgeance, which is why it is a good time to get ahead and learn about what NFTs even are in the first place. There is a lot of misinformation and misinterperatation going around the common folk about NFTs and what they are, as well as what they truly mean. Lets dive into it.

WHAT IS AN NFT?

So lets first discuss what an NFT actually is. For starters, NFTs stand for Non-Fungible Tokens, which are digital assets and collectibles that are non- interchangeable as no NFT is alike with another, resulting in NFTs having a unique value. Unlike Bitcoin or Ether(the native cryptocurrency coin to the Ethereum Blockchain Network) which are fungible coins as the value between their currencies will never change, NFTs cannot be swapped with the same value as every single NFT in the market is completely unique and digitally scarce. NFTs are essentially just pieces of code(a smart contract) that is owned and held by a specific address. This address will possess a digital passkey that will point to a server that will host an image, gif, etc that this address owns. So it isnt aboout the collectible itself neccessarily, but the code that runs it. Pretty simple and straightforward!

AUTHENTICITY AND OWNERSHIP

Lets get into a major part of NFT technology and a part that many of the common folk seem to miss(not hating just pointing it out), and NFTs are not necessarilty about the collectible itself, it is about the code and how it grants ownership of that specific digital asset while ensuring its secrurity and authenticity. See when you prchase an NFT, you are essentially buying the rights to own that NFT, and when you "mint" an NFT(which simply means to generate or create a NFT of your own), you are generating a unique digital token with a unique digital token ID. Whenever you mint, buy, or sell an NFT, that transaction is recorded and timestamped the Blockchain with the information of the addresses that the NFT, minted the NFT, and sold the NFT. Meaning that anyone can verify the authenticity and ownership of an NFT be going to the Blockchain and checking for themselves as all activity on the Blockchain, including NFTs, is traceable. It is fairly simple and easy to check authenticity as only those that own the real and authentic NFT can actually sell the NFT, and that even if someone were to lets say take a screenshot(a common arguement people have against NFTs) of said NFT, it would not mean anything as they are not the true and real owners of that NFT which again, is a unique token ID. This entire system is crucial as it protects owners and creators from purchasing and transfering forgeries and fraud, and ensures that participants are buying and selling genuine and authentic pieces.

BENEFITS FOR ARTISTS

NFTs grant a new path for artists of different backgrounds and styles to bring their imagination to life while also being able to benefit from Blockchain technology. However in the beautiful world of digital art, many artists can face some very challenging and often times stress inducing obstacles. One of these being monetization. In a digital world, it can be quite difficult for artists to ensure their work is not being tampered with, copied, stolen and used for monetary gain while they just sit back and deal with it, while also not even gaining royalities and or compensation for such things. This is a very frustrating and stressful thing that unfortunately many digital artists deal with, not to mention how difficult it can be for many artists to break into the industry in the first place. However, with NFTs and Blockchain, we can help artists combat these obstacles and make their lives a bit easier.

NFTs and Blockchain would help artists counter the scary obstacle that is monetization by allowing artists, creators, musicians, writers etc to easily monetize, own, and distribute their work online through ownership and authencity. An artist would easily be able to trace and track where their art goes and where it ends up. This creates a level playing field for artists and allows them to headline their work on a global scale and helps them break into the art industry in a safe and more secure way. NFTs allow artists to actually have real ownership of their creations while gaining compensation for their work and protecting their property. By allowing creators and artists control and ownership over their work, they cant set the terms and conditions as to how their work can be used or sold and to ensure that their work is not being appropriated and used without their permission.

ART

As mentioned plenty of times earlier in this article, NFTs can and are used as digital art. Digital art is the largest and most mainstream use case when it comes down to NFTs and for good reason. NFTs can be used as a medium for art as artists can mint NFTs to represent their own work such as paintings, drawings, collages, images etc, and their own creative work with Blockchain Technology in order to create a unique form of digital art. Just like more traditional forms of digital art, NFT Art has an infinite range as you can encompass whatever art style and design as you wish.

MUSIC

Many people believe that the only use case for NFTs is digital art, and the reality that NFTs can be used for far more and a great example would be music. The music industry is clearly a large entertainment sector and is very prominent in society and culture. However, many do not realize that music artists don't have it easy. The reality is that the vast majority of artists dont have full rights and ownership over their own work, and a large percentage of the revenue stream they receive goes to their record labels rather than their own pockets. This has led to music artists exploring the world of NFTs for a few reasons thsu the creationsof Music NFTs.

Music NFTs are NFTs that are issued using Blockchain technology and are linked to a specific song, album, EP, video etc. Artists can use these NFTs to represent their music, concert tickets, merchandise, virtual experiences etc to allow their fans and community to buy, sell, trade and own something that is directly linked to their favorite artists. Purchasing these NFTs can be a way for fans to support their favorite artists while allowing artists to be more in tune with their fans and generate their own revenue. It allows music artists to make an imprint on the blockchain while truly preserving their ownership of their own music for good, and also giving their fans a direct connection to their work and craft.

PFPs

A very popular type of NFT, Picture For Profile NFTS or PFP NFTs for short, are exclusive digital images designed to serve as their owners profile picture of digital identity in a blockchain ecosystem. In the real world, we each have our own digital identiy we go by and in order for us to conduct many of our daily tasks, we must have identification in order for us to conduct these tasks. IDs also allow us to access certain communities, clubs, events etc otherwise we wouldnt be able to do so. You see a PFP NFT does the same thing....however it takes it to the digital world e.x Metaverse. PFPs allow users and participants to create their own digital idenities while granting them access to certain groups of their choosing and or liking. PFP NFTs can also allow users to choose profiles that speak to them and represent their own unique personality! However there is another type of PFP NFT that users can create...or should I say generate.

Generative PFP NFTs are NFTs that are generate using a set of rules and algorithms that the creator specifies using code and artificial intelligence. A creator can set the parameters of these NFTs such as the color, shape, size, animations, etc and this library of characteristics will then be executed by the algorithm, which will then produce a collection of images and or animations based on the characteristics set by the creator. Generative PFPs allow users to create large collections(up to 10,000 NFTs) in a faster manner comapred to doing it all one by one, while also giving each NFT its own character and unique qualities.

GAMING

Gaming NFTs are NFTs that are associated with any digital item that is used in a gaming ecosystem and enviornment. This inlcudes characters, skins, maps, points, weapons, customizations, different game modes, anything that is used within a gaming enviornment. Gaming NFTs, just like with any other type of NFT, allow the user to authentically own and manage their purchases and items. One problem the gaming world and its gamers face today is the facilitation of in game items and purchases. The majority of video games today do not allow for purchases or aquisitions in a game to be transferable to other game enviornements. Your ownership of that item is soley adapted to the specific game itself, and once the game goes, so do your items and purchases as they are non transferable, which of course means you have to start over. Another thing to note or keep in mind is that your in game items and purchases can be stripped away from you by the developers and company behind the game, and you really wouldnt be able to do much about it.

Gaming NFTs can offer provable ownership of in game item purchases and allow gamers to buy, sell, and trade these items as due to the unique nature of NFTs, said items have digital scarcity and provide value and potential monetary gain. Another potential benefit to Gaming NFTs is the potential interopability between different gaming ecosystems and enviornements. Meaning one day we could potential have Mario racing Sonic in the same game enviornement, pretty neat! Now keep in mind Gaming NFTs are a big work in progress, and they got a long way to go before it gets established as a strong sector within this industry. However, the potential is most certainly there and it is quite interesting to think about how far it is going to go.

MEMBERSHIP

Membership NFTs grant users access to clubs, communities, utilities, ecosystems etc in the digital world of Blockchain Technology, just like a real world membership plan, pass or ID. Membership NFTs have been getting some traction lately and can be incorporated into multiple ecosystems within the world of Blockchain and even other NFT types. And by of course utilizing Blockchain technology and smart contracts, Membership NFTs can provide some real world benefits and utility. By replacing traditional paper based memebrship cards with an NFT, users and owners can store said NFT in their crypto wallet and use that NFT as a ID or passcode to get into a exclusive club or community, while also allowing businesses and companies to charge upfront costs or micro payments for access to their club, all being done by smart contracts(see "What is Blockchain" article). Membership NFTs also eliminate the potential or fraud and forgery as instead of relying on passcodes, physical cards, and documents, all of which are forged and copied on a daily basis, NFTs allow users to store their membership and ID digitally on the Blockchain in a safe and secure way using cryptography in the form of encrypted tokens. On top of that, if a company wishes to validate the authenticity of a memebrship, they can easily do so by looking at the blockchain for themselves.

PHOTOGRAPHY

Photography NFTs are pretty straightfoward and simple. They are photographs taken by photographers that are represented by an NFT and put on the Blockchain just like any other NFT. Photgraphy NFTs are one of the newest kinds of NFTs in the market and allow Photgraphers, just like any other artists using NFTs, to gain true monetization over their work.

DOMAINS

Domain NFTs function pretty similar to a domain name in the traditional web while utilizing Blockchain Technology. In the web world, whenever you wish to deploy a website to the internet, one of the first things you must do is buy a domain name from a host. Of course just like many other things that I have already mentioned, you do not truly own this domain name and have to go through a Domain Name System, This of course is a concern for bloggers, business owners, creators etc as their property can be stripped away from them, and also while they are under this domain name, their personal data is a bit of a concern. With a Domain NFT, your data is stored on the blockchain and these domain names translate into wallet addresses, and is essentially used as a shortcut to your wallet. Domain NFTs allow users to own their data on the web and helps them avoid going through third party services like a Domain Name System and allows users to truly take control of the domain they aquired.
