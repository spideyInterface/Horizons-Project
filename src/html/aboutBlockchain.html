WHAT IS HORIZONS

JUNE 12th, 2024

Lets get into it,

Now I am sure many of you might be wondering what Blockchain is in the first place and how it works and what it means for you and your pockets. After all, you are spending funds in order to create, trade, sell and purchase these NFTs, so I am sure you are eager and tempted to understand and get a grip on what the technology that even makes this posissible is in the first place. Well, here you go.

WHAT IS BLOCKCHAIN?

Blockchain Technology is a distributed ledger and database that stores transactions, information, and data into bundles of records called blocks. These blocks are then chained in chronological order in order to form an immuatable and tamper proof database. Blockchain runs on a decentralized peer to peer network, meaning instead of there being one central authority that is governing the entire network, the network is made up of many different participants that are spread across the globe and these participants are refered to as nodes. Each node has an equal responsibility in maintaining the secrurity and stability of the network while also being responsible for validating and verifying pieces of information, data and transactions being transfered across the network.

WHY BLOCKCHAIN?

Blockchain does not function the same as other more traditional databases. As stated already, blockchains are not owned by a single entity as the power and computation is spread across multiple participants and because of this, Blockchain encompassses some very interesting and downright great technology. Lets get into it!

CONSENSUS

Consensus plays a very vital role in how Blockchains operate and function, and is the sole reason why all these nodes participating in the network can even validate and very transactions within unit in the first place. Consensus is the process of coming to an agreement in order to make a decision. And in Blockchain, that means that in order for a new transaction and or piece of data to be put onto the main chain, all of the participating nodes within the network must first come to a agreement and conclusion that this piece of data is authorized to be on chain. Once these nodes within the network verify this piece of data to be secure and authorized, it is then validated, put into a block, and then recorded on the blockchain.

There are two types of consensus mechanisms/systems that are currently used in Blockchain Technology. First we have the original blockchain consensus mechanism(and the most outdated)which is named Proof of Work which was introduced by Bitcoin back in 2009. In a Proof of Work based network, all of the participating nodes within the network are called miners. These miners are using their computational power and energy resources to solve a very complex mathematical equation/algorithm which is attempting to solve the hash to the new block being added. Whichever miner solves this mathematical algorithm first, mines the new block onto the chain and gets rewarded for it. This is the process of mining. Although Proof of Work is a fairly secure architectural structure for Blockchain, it does have some drawbacks. The main drawback being it is very energy intensive and resource demanding. See these miners that are mining new blocks onto the chain arent small pieces of software. They are massive pieces of machinery(and thousands of them running at once) that are using laods of energy to solve a very complex mathematical equation taht requires a ton of computational power. Due to this, many blockchains are moving away from Proof of Work and to the new kid on the block....Proof of Stake.

The next and newest consensus mechanism is called Proof of Stake. Proof of Stake was introduced by Ethereum back in 2022 to solve the energy and scalibility problem Proof of Work was facing. In a Proof of Stake network, the nodes running the network are validators instead of miners. What does that mean? Well its fairly simple and straightforward. In a Proof of Stake architecture, the network will pick validators based on how many native coins or tokens participants stake within the blockchain ecosystem. Whichever participants are staking the most coins/tokens, gets picked as a validator and gets rewarded staking rewards. Because there is no mathematical equation involved, the energy needed to compute and validate these transactions and data gets reduced significantly while allowing the transaction speed to increase. Now you are probably wondering, how do you even stake in the first place? Well its actually fairly simple and far easier them you think! All you have to do is choose an exchange, then choose a coin/token that is based on a Proof of Stake architecture, and aquire a specified amount of coins or tokens according to the requirements of the protocol, and then lock up those funds into that protocol for a set period of time. Once you get picked as a validator in the network, you will start to earn staking rewards. This is essentially compensation that the Blockchain is giving you for allowing the network to use your coins/tokens to facilitate and run the network! Its a win win!

SMART CONTRACTS

Smart Contracts are one of the core fundamental aspects of Blockchain Technology and plays a vital role in not only the functionality of Blockchain, but also what it can do. Smart Contracts are complex pieces of code and software progam that are built alongside a blockchain network. Once the predetermined conditions of this contractual agreement are met, the contract and code is automatically executed. It is a way to eliminate the need for outside third party services and intermediaries to manage and oversee data, information, funds accounts, contracts etc and moving from a trust based system in which you have to trust a third party service to handle data, to a fully autonomous system once the contract is deployed onto the blockchain.

Practically everything you see in Blockchain has been touched by a smart contract, interacts with one, or IS a Smart Contract and this includes NFTs! Yes the NFT you are eyeing or have purchased is in fact a smart contract built using a specific standard, but more about that in the "What is an NFT" page.

DECENTRALIZATION

As mentioned earlier, decentralization is a integral part of blockchain technology, and allows databases to operate in a trustless manner in which anyone and everyone participating in the network can have access to the same real time data and information. It distributes the responsibility and power evently amongst network participants and because of this, the network becomes a very robust and secure databse. However despite all of this, keep in mind that not all data has to be stored in a blockchain and that blockchain has a spectrum of decentralization, So choose wisely!

SECURITY

Blockchain Technology is a very secure and stable databse and this comes down to a few reasons. For starters, since all the data within a Blockchain network is distributed among multiple participants instead of just one central authority, there is no central point of failure which in turn makes it very difficult for these networks to be attacked and also makes it very difficult for an entire Blockchain network to go down( which is why Blockchain runs 24/7/no downtime). Another component that makes Blockchain very robust is the fact that it utilizes cryptographic principles with Asymmetric Key Encryption, which allows data to be held and sent in a secure and safe manner while ensuring data secrurity and authentication.

IMMUTABILITY

All Blockchain Technology, no matter if its Public, Private, Hybrid, Proof of Stake, Proof of Work, Ethereum, Bitcoin, Ripple and so on and so forth, is immutable and tamper proof. Whenever data gets validated onto the chain, it is stored there forever as is. You cannot change, delete tamper with, or alter data and information that has been added to a block on chain as each block is encrypted with its data finalized. This ensures that all activity on the Blockchain gets recorded making Blockchain a reliable and dependant database to store various kinds of information. You cannot lie in Blockchain as anyone can go check the activity for themselves. Honesty is the best policy.

TRUSTLESS

Since Blockchain is ran by different nodes participating in the network with computation and software, the network does not require you to gain any type of permission in order to view or interact with the Blockchain as most Blockchains are censorship free with no gatekeepers choosing who can participate and access the network. Now keep in mind not all blockchains are completely decentralized and open source, as some blockchain networks are Private therefore have a preselected amount of users that control and govern the entire network, and do not allow those outside of this group to access or oversee the chain. Some Blockchain networks run on a Hybrid Blockchain which consists of Public and Private techniques and provides a middle ground as a Hybrid network tends to be ran by one authority or pre selected users, however incorporates some decentralization processes within the network via applications, smart contracts etc. Nevertheless, most Blockchain networks out there are open source databases, in which anyone and everyone can access and interact with the network while also being able to see the activity going on within the databse.
