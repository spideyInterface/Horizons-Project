    What is a crypto wallet

    Crypto Wallet Basics

    When it comes down to conducting transactions in crypto world, we all need a place to store our funds and required information in order to conduct these transactions in the network, and this where wallets come in, in particular...... crypto wallets. In the real world, we use wallets to store our mediums of exchange such as credits cards, debit cards, and cash along with our IDs such as our drivers license and membership cards. In the crypto world, we do the same, except our wallets are digital and have some technical differences because of that. Lets get into it!

    What exactly is a crypto wallet?

    So lets first discuss what a crypto wallet actually is and what it actually stores. Believe it or not, a crypto wallet doesnt actually store cryptocurrency...SHOCKER! You see your funds are actually stored on the blockchain itself, and your crypto wallet stores the digital passkeys needed for you to ACCCESS your funds on that blockchain. These two digital pass keys are known as your public key and your private key. Whats that mean for you? Well lets see.

    What is cryptography?

    Before we get into what a public and private key are, we first must understand what cryptography is as a whole, and no, cryptography is not jsut specific to crypto and Blockchain despite having the word crypto in it. Cryptography has been in the tech and web developement world for a long time now, and plays a vital role in how we manage to send and receive information and data across databases and servers in a safe and secure manner. Cryptography is the art and science of converting human readable text into computer code, with the sole purpose and reason of ensuring information and data does not get exploited and tampered with by anyone who isnt validated to see that pieces of information and or message. Cryptography comes down to two essential techniques, them being encryption and decryption.

    Encryption is the process of converting human readable text called plain text, into computer code called cipher text, with the purpose of ensuring anyone who doesnt have authorization to see that message or piece of information can access it. The only way to see an encrypted message is by utilization of a digital passkey to that message, and this digital passkey will be held and used by the recipient of that message. This digital passkey will decrypt the message. What is decryption? Decryption is the opposite of encryption, in which in decryption, it is the process of converting computer code, which again, is called cipher text, back into its original form which is human readable text, which again, is called plain text. These two techniques ensure that information and data can be sent around networks in a safe and secure manner, and to ensure that we as users can be confident in storing and sending said information whenever we are dealing with databases and servers. That said, not all databases and networks are created equal and not all have secrurity standards such as cryptography up to par, so make sure you are getting involved with reputable and strong databases and networks. In Blockchain, we use two different types of cryptography; Asymetric Key Encryption(also known as Public Key Cryptography) and Hashing Functions.

    Asymetric Key Encryption is when two digital passkeys are required to encrypt and decrypt a message and unlike Symetric Key Encryption(also known as Private Key Cryptography) which you use your private key to both encrypt and decrypt a message, these two digital passkeys are not interchangeable and you cannot use one in replacement for the other. In order to encrypt a message, you use your public key. In order to decrypt a message, you use your private key.

    Along with Asymetric Key Encryption, we also use hashing functions as a form of cryptography. Hashing is the process of using a mathematical equation or algorithm to convert an input of arbitrary length into an output of a fixed length, and the same data and input will always produce the same output and value. Since hashes are a fixed lenght, its nearly impossible for a bad actor to guess the hash if someone was trying to tamper with a blockchain, and since the input of a hash will always produce the same output, everytime a bad actor changes anything about the hash of a block, the entire hash itself will change entirely, further ensuring its secrurity and robustness. Alogn with all this, hashing makes data location easier to manage.

    What is a public key?

    Public keys are the digital passkeys we use to encrypt a transaction that is sent to a recipient across the network, in order to ensure it is secure and unexploitable. Along with this, in the world of Blockchain, our public key is what we use to verify the digital signature of the sender for the transaction. A public key is publicly viewable and used information that anyone can access and utilize. Think of a public key as your username or email address. Anyone that has access to these pieces of information can send funds or information to your account, however they will not be able to actually access your account. This is where your private key comes in.

    What is a private key?

    Public keys are the digital passkeys we use to encrypt a transaction that is sent to a recipient across the network, in order to ensure it is secure and unexploitable. Along with this, in the world of Blockchain, our public key is what we use to verify the digital signature of the sender for the transaction. A public key is publicly viewable and used information that anyone can access and utilize. Think of a public key as your username or email address. Anyone that has access to these pieces of information can send funds or information to your account, however they will not be able to actually access your account. This is where your private key comes in.

    You can think of private keys as your password or secure login information. You wouldn't go around showing people the password to your bank account would you? Would you go around showing the password to your cashapp account? Or email address? I would hope not. Thats because this is sensible information that only you are supposed to be able to access and see, and anyone that has access to this information will be able to access the information and funds to your account. So private keys, as the name suggests, are meant to only be seen by you and should never be seen by anyone else other than yourself. So never, ever, ever share your private key! Along with keeping your private key safe, you should also keep your seed phrase safe. Whenever you create a new crypto wallet, it will generate a unique seed phrase for you to copy and write down, as it is a human readable representation of your private key and is used as a mnemonic backup phrase to gain access to your account and wallet whenever you lose your wallet. In case you do lose your wallet, your seed phrase will regenerate your private key and all the funds in your wallet. So keep your seed phrase stored safely and practice remembering it!

    Types of wallets?

    Software

    Software wallets are arguably the most widely used and known type of crypto wallets out there. A software wallet is a digital extension and or application that is always connected to the internet, providing ease of access to by, sell, trade, stake, borrow, lend, and hold cryptocurrencies and NFTs. These wallets are regarded as "cold" wallets as they always depend on internet connection in order to run. However, because these wallets are always connected to the internet as they inherently need internet connection in order to operate, they are also the most succeptible to online hacks and attacks. So although these attacks are still generally rare, it is something to keep in mind. The most popular software crypto wallet in the world is Metamask. Metamask is an Ethereum based blockchain wallet that runs in your browser, and provides an easy and convienient way to store and handle ERC tokens and coins.

    Hardware

    Hardware wallets are regarded to be the most secure and stable wallet to hold your private keys, and these wallets are regarded as "hot walelts". A hardware wallet is a physical device that stores private keys and funds both offline and online. Unlike software wallets, which need internet connection in order to operate, hardware wallets do not need web connection in order to effectively stor your data. Your funds and private keys are safe and secure within these wallets as it is impossible for a hacker or bad actor to exploit a wallet that is not connected to the internet, and along with that, these wallets require you to sign in whenever it is actually being utilized. In order to conduct transactions or update the hardware, you simply connect your hardware wallet to your computer, sign in using your passkey and carry on as normal. Once you disconnect your hardware wallet form your computer, the walelt automatically disconnects from your computer and the internet, ensuring your data is secure. The two obvious downsides to a hardware wallet are that it is physical, meaning it can be physically lost and or broken. And two, that it is not as convienient and fast to use as a software wallet, as the initial login requires more work and steps on your part. Still, so long as you remember and or keep your seed phrase and passkey safe and hidden, you would be able to regenerate your data and private keys if you do manage to lose your hardhware wallet.

    Paper

    Paper keys are very simple and straightforward. Paper keys are physical pieces of paper that users will either print or write their private and public keys, along with the addresses and seed phrases that come with their account. Downsides are pretty obvious and quite drastic. For starters, you can physically lose the paper and thereofre lose access to your account for good. On top of that, the ink of the paper can fade over time making it more and more difficult to read and understand and you also run the risk of having your paper get caught in bad weather such as rain or snow, making it unreadable. Although paper wallets are technically secure, they may not be the most up to date and best modern choice for storing such precious information such as your keys and seed phrase.
